Responsible Disclosure Policy

Our commitment to ethical security research and coordinated vulnerability disclosure.

Our Commitment

TracerBody is committed to working with security researchers and the broader cybersecurity community to identify and address security vulnerabilities through responsible disclosure practices. We believe that coordinated vulnerability disclosure is essential for improving security across the digital ecosystem.

This policy outlines our approach to receiving, investigating, and addressing security vulnerability reports while protecting both researchers and affected organizations.

Scope

This responsible disclosure policy applies to security vulnerabilities discovered in:

In Scope

• TracerBody websites and web applications

• TracerBody-developed security tools and software

• TracerBody research infrastructure

• Third-party systems used by TracerBody

• Open source projects maintained by TracerBody

Out of Scope

• Social engineering attacks

• Physical security issues

• Denial of service attacks

• Issues in third-party services beyond our control

• Vulnerabilities requiring physical access

Reporting Process

How to Report

To report a security vulnerability, please send an email to:

security@tracerbody.org

For sensitive reports, you may encrypt your message using our PGP key available on our website.

Required Information

Please include the following information in your vulnerability report:

• Detailed description of the vulnerability

• Steps to reproduce the issue

• Potential impact and severity assessment

• Affected systems, applications, or components

• Proof of concept (if applicable and safe)

• Your contact information for follow-up

• Any additional context or recommendations

Response Timeline

Initial Response

Within 24 hours of receiving your report

Investigation

5-10 business days for initial assessment

Resolution

30-90 days depending on complexity

Disclosure

Coordinated with researcher and affected parties

Research Guidelines

Acceptable Research

We encourage security research that follows these guidelines:

• Only test against systems you own or have explicit permission to test

• Avoid accessing, modifying, or deleting data belonging to others

• Respect user privacy and data confidentiality

• Minimize impact on system availability and performance

• Do not perform testing that could harm users or systems

• Report vulnerabilities promptly and responsibly

• Maintain confidentiality until coordinated disclosure

Prohibited Activities

The following activities are not permitted under this policy:

• Accessing or modifying data that doesn't belong to you

• Disrupting or degrading system performance

• Social engineering attacks against employees or users

• Physical attacks against facilities or equipment

• Violating applicable laws or regulations

• Public disclosure before coordinated disclosure timeline

• Demanding payment or compensation for vulnerability reports

Legal Protection

TracerBody is committed to protecting security researchers who act in good faith and follow this responsible disclosure policy.

Our Commitment

• We will not pursue legal action against researchers who follow this policy

• We will work with researchers to understand and address reported vulnerabilities

• We will provide credit and recognition for valid vulnerability reports

• We will maintain confidentiality of researcher information

Researcher Responsibilities

• Follow all guidelines outlined in this policy

• Act in good faith and avoid malicious activities

• Respect the coordinated disclosure timeline

• Comply with applicable laws and regulations

Recognition

We believe in recognizing the valuable contributions of security researchers to improving cybersecurity for everyone.

Hall of Fame

Researchers who report valid vulnerabilities will be listed in our security researcher hall of fame (with their permission) and credited in our security advisories.

Public Recognition

We may publicly recognize significant contributions to our security through blog posts, conference presentations, and other appropriate channels.

Research Collaboration

Outstanding researchers may be invited to collaborate on future security research projects or participate in our security advisory board.
